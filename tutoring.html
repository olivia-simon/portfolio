The Art of Editing

“In the end, what makes a book valuable is not the paper it’s printed on, but the thousands of hours of work by dozens of people who are dedicated to creating the best possible reading experience for you.”
― John Green

The hardest thing in writing is getting perspective. We become so locking into our own ideas that we forget how to make them accessible to others. We become over attached to phrases, picky about our sentence structures, and slowly, we lose sight of the "big picture."

And that's where I come in. As a veteran writing tutor and editor for The Writing Center at Washington University in St. Louis, I have developed a strategy to improve your writing no matter your level or subject matter. Check out my method below!

Step 1: Identifying Structural Issues

One of the most frequent problems I see in papers (and writing in general) is that the thesis (or purpose, in non-academic settings) gets lost or obscured by information that doesn't support it. In identifying structural issues, we ensure that your writing says what it intends to say, and does so in the most clear and effective way possible. We will make sure what you're saying and how you're saying it is as clear as possible, and that you're actively supporting your main argument.

Step 2: Identifying Surface Level Issues

Do dangling participles plague your sentneces? Are you choosing the correct words? Is your puncuation and citation style correct? Do you have run-ons? Are you properly connecting independent clauses? Do your tenses change inconsistenly? Writing is often riddled with these sorts of surface level problems, so in any editing session, I make sure that we address major issues and learn how to correct them.

Step 3: Synthesizing and Revising

Truthfully, in writing we could Steps 1 and 2 forever...but at some point you'll have to call it a day. This step of the editing process brings together the structural and the surface level corrections to create a new draft. This is our end goal: a more polished, coherent, draft that synthesizing all the edits in a meaningful way. This step also solidifies your understanding of the edits and strengthens your writing process.

I'm not just about editing, I'm about giving you the tools to become a more effetive writer. So let's start working together now - the first session is on me.
